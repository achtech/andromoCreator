<div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>1. Remove the wooden window frame from the window, if possible.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/6/67/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-17.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-17.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Removing the screen and working on a flat surface will be much easier than attempting to work with the screen while it is still in the window.</div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>2. Pry the molding out of the window that's holding the screen in place.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/5/5f/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-18.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-18.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Use a thin pry bar to gently pull up the molding. It's important to be gentle because you want to be able to use the molding again.[17]  </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>Tap on the end of the pry bar with a hammer to help work it under the molding.</li><li>Once you get the molding out, tap the nails on the pointed end and remove them with a hammer.</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>3. Take the old screen off the frame.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/1/13/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-19.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-19.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Use the claw side of a hammer or pry bar to pull out the staples or nails that hold the screen in place. As you do, the screen should start to come off the frame. Pull it up, and set it aside to discard it later.[18] </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>If any nails or staples are left in the frame after you remove the screen, pry them out.[19]</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>4. Cut a piece of screen to fit the frame.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/2/20/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-20.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-20.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Leave at least 1 to 2 inches (2.5 to 5.1 cm) of extra screen on each side of the frame. It's better to cut more than you need than to have to cut it again because you don't have enough.[20] </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>You should have at least at least 2 sides that meet at a right angle. That will make it easier to attach the screen.[21]</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>5. Starting stapling or nailing at one edge of the right angle.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/8/8d/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-21.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-21.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Lay the right angle of the screen into a corner, fitting it into the grooves for the screen, and staple it on the short side of the corner. Staple the other side of the corner into place, and start working along that long edge, pulling the screen taut as you go.[22]  </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>Add staples or nails every 1 inch (2.5 cm) or so.</li><li>Make sure the staples are as flat as possible against the frame.</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>6. Set the top in place with staples or nails.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/f/fc/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-22.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-22.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Go along the short side connected to the right angle. Pull the screen tight as you go across the edge, stapling every 1 inch (2.5 cm) or so.[23]</div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>7. Staple the opposite long edge in place.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/9/90/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-23.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-23.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Pull the screen taut across the frame. Start stapling from the short edge that's already stapled in place. Make sure the screen is tight going across and down the frame as you staple.[24]</div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>8. Attach the bottom edge.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/4/40/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-24.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-24.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Stretch the screen as much as you can across the frame, then staple or nail it into the bottom corner. Run staples or nails across the bottom edge.[25] </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>With this step, you should finish stapling the screen into place.</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>9. Trim the screen using a utility knife.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/5/52/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-25.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-25.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Run a utility knife along the inside edge of the frame. Try not to cut the wood behind it with the knife. Trim the 2 sides that have excess screen, and discard it.[26] </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>You can trim the screen earlier if it's in the way while you're stapling. You should only have to trim the screen on 2 sides if you used a right angle.</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>10. Put the molding back in place.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/b/b4/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-26.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-26.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Lay the pieces back down where they belong. Use nails to drive them back into place. Create new holes, or use larger nails in the old holes.[27] </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>If a piece broke, you can glue it back together or buy a new piece of molding.</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'>
