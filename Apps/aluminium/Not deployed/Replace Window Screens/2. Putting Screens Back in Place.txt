Pay attention when you take the screens out.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/d/d0/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-12.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-12.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> Some screens only go back in one way, and if you don't put them back in the same way, they aren't going to stay. Look at the screen when you're pulling it out, and mark the top of the screen if you need to.  </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>If you forgot to look at the screen when you took it down, pull out another one to see how it goes in place.</li><li>The spline will always face outward.</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>2. Start from the top.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/4/49/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-13.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-13.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> It's usually easiest to begin from the top of the window and work your way down. However, as you slide it into place, make sure the pressure clips are falling into the proper track on the right. Most often, the springs should go to the right when looking at the window from the outside.[13]</div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>3. Check the springs or pressure clips if it won't go in.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/0/05/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-14.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-14.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> These springs look like little metal strips that create a curve on one edge. Sometimes, the clips pop out of place. They should be against the outside edge only, not flopping to the left or right.[14]</div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>4. Place 2-part screens in like a puzzle.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/e/e7/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-15.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-15.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> The top screen will have a lip at the bottom that goes over the bottom screen. The pressure clips, if the screen has them, need to be to the right when looking at the window from the outside. Press the pressure clips into the track on the right, then press in the left top corner. Push the screen to the top without pressing in the bottom left corner.[15] </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>Insert the second screen under the top screen's bottom lip with the pressure clips facing right. Push the screen all the way over to the right. Press the bottom left corner in, while still keeping the top screen's bottom corner and the bottom screen's top corner out a bit. Once the bottom left corner is in, press on the left middle part where the corners meet to set it in place.</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'><div style='max-width:100%'><p><b style='color: rgb(128, 0, 0);'>5. Set in a 1-part screen with the springs to the left from the inside.</b></p><div><center><img src='https://www.wikihow.com/images/thumb/3/33/Replace-Window-Screens-Step-16.jpg/aid1381431-v4-728px-Replace-Window-Screens-Step-16.jpg'  style='width: 80%; margin-right:10%; border: 2px solid; border-radius: 25%; margin-bottom: 10px;' /></center></div><div style='width: 90%; margin-left: 5%; text-align: justify'> If you need to put the screen in from the inside, the springs should be on the left. The feathered/pleated edge will go on the top. Slide the top part of the screen out the window, and bring it back flat against the window. Set the screen in the tracks with the springs in place. Lift the screen up the window, and set it in place from the top if you can.[16] </div><div><ul style='padding-bottom: 8px;width: 80%;text-align: justify;'><li>To set it in place from the top, press in towards the house at the top corners. You can do this from the outside, or if your house has windows that open from the top and bottom, from the inside.</li></ul></div><hr style='height: 2px;color: rgb(128, 0, 0);background-color: rgb(128, 0, 0);border: none;'>
